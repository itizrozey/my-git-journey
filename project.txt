Inventory Management System for an automobile system/warehouse:

    System Overview:
        The system will manage the inventory of automobile parts/products in a ware house, tracking products with attributes

        name: Product name (i.e, engin oil, break pads, etc)
        category: Product category (e.g, Engine, Breakes, etc)
        quantity: Current stock quantity
        price: Product price
        supplier: Product supplier

        The system will have the following features: 
        1. Add stock: Increase the quantity of a product in stock
        2. Sell Items: Reduce the quantity of a product in stock
        3. Low-Stock Alerts: Notify when a product's quantity falls below a certain threshold
        4. Update Product: Add a new product to the stock
        5. Delete Product: Remove a particular product from the stock
        6. Sales Report by Period: Generate a report of sales for a given period (e.g., daily, weekly, monthly)
        7. View Product: To see the entire stock from from the database here 


Problem statement:
    The Context:
        Traditional automobile warehouses and spare part dealerships often rely on manual ledgers or basic spreadsheets to track high-value inventory. In an industry where a single engine block can cost millions and a missing spark plug can stall a repair, precision is non-negotiable.

    The Challenges (The "Problems"):
        Inventory Inaccuracy: Manual tracking leads to "Ghost Stock" (items listed as available but physically missing) or "Stockouts" (running out of critical parts like brake pads), causing lost sales and customer frustration.

        Lack of Real-Time Alerts: Without an automated system, managers only realize an item is out of stock when they go to fetch it. There is no proactive notification when a part hits its minimum threshold.

        Financial Blind Spots: Calculating the "Total Inventory Value" (the capital tied up in the warehouse) is a slow, error-prone process when done manually, making it difficult to assess the business's health.

        Disconnected Sales Records: Sales are often recorded separately from stock updates. This "de-coupling" makes it impossible to generate accurate Sales Reports by Period or to verify if a sale was even possible based on current stock levels. 

This system reduces Human Error by 40-60% compared to manual entry—this makes the "Problem" sound much more urgent to solve!

The Solution:
This project develops a Centralized Inventory Management System using Python and MySQL. The system bridges the gap between the warehouse and the sales desk by: 
    Enforcing Data Integrity (preventing sales of non-existent stock).

    Automating Low-Stock Alerts to ensure high-demand parts are always reordered.

    Generating Dynamic Financial Reports that provide instant insights into revenue and total asset value through a structured database.
